Diego Maradona

Argentine footballer

Diego Maradona playing for FC Barcelona

Here's a time line of Diego Maradona's life:

  • 1960: Born on October 30 in Villa Fiorito, Buenos Aires, Argentina.
  • 1976: Debuts for Argentinos Juniors at age 15.
  • 1977: Makes his debut for the Argentina national team.
  • 1981: Transfers to Boca Juniors, winning the Argentine Primera División.
  • 1982: Moves to FC Barcelona for a then-world-record fee.
  • 1984: Joins Napoli, leading them to their first Serie A title in 1987 and another in 1990, as well as a UEFA Cup victory in 1989.
  • 1982: Plays in his first FIFA World Cup.
  • 1986: Captains Argentina to World Cup victory in Mexico, famously scoring the "Hand of God" goal and the "Goal of the Century" against England in the quarterfinals.
  • 1990: Leads Argentina to the World Cup final in Italy, finishing as runners-up to West Germany.
  • 1994: Competes in his final World Cup but is sent home after failing a drug test.
  • 1992-1997: Plays for Sevilla, Newell's Old Boys, and finally returns to Boca Juniors before retiring.
  • 2008: Becomes head coach of the Argentina national team, leading them to the 2010 World Cup quarterfinals.
  • 2000: Publishes his autobiography "Yo Soy El Diego."
  • 2001: FIFA names him one of the two greatest players in history, alongside Pelé.
  • 2020: Passes away on November 25 due to a heart attack. Napoli renames their stadium Stadio Diego Armando Maradona in his honor.
